Ground Software

With $79.5 mil in funding from NASA, Iris, a 2.5 kg lunar rover, is expected to launch in 2021. It will be the first unmanned private lander on the Moon’s surface. As Image Viewer Lead, I am responsible for facilitating the execution and release of Image Viewer, or the eyes of the rover.

Organization

Iris Rover

Project Year

2020

Role

Image Viewer Lead

The Challenge

Iris’ ground software teleoperates the lunar rover from Earth to carry out the mission. Our goal is to design the ground systems interface and experience, driven by both human-centered and mission requirements.

Design Ecosystem

Teleoperations is divided into four areas: Command Line, Map, Telemetry, and Image Viewer. Together, the rover can traverse and document the Moon’s surface.

Image Viewer Goal

The Image Viewer must extract as much meaningful information from the little data we get from the moon as possible. It also must display the information to the operators in the most efficient and intelligible way. Finally, captured images should be organized to be easily accessed by the Science team post-mission.


I was onboarded for the homestretch before the rover’s launch, focusing component revision, user testing, interaction refinement, and documentation for the Operations Guideline.
