Dashboard

Spokeo strived to add value to their thousands of subscribers through various search types. The dashboard would educate users about the different search types as well as introduce Identity Protection.

Organization

Spokeo

Project Year

2020

Role

UI/UX Designer

The Challenge

How can we build a “stickier” product and improve user engagement?


Spokeo was crippled by the “search paradox”—that is, users conducted one search and cancelled their subscription shortly after. How could Spokeo increase the visibility of their various services and increase retention of their subscribers?

Project Goals

After gathering stakeholder requirements, I outlined the objectives as follows:


— Increase trial-to-sub conversion rate

— Increase retention of subscribers

— Educate users about the various search types

— Introduce Identity Protection

Preliminary Research

I conducted semi-structured interviews and directed storytelling with several customer care agents to better understand subscriber frustrations.


1. Customers are unaware of what their subscription includes.

2. Customers have difficulty finding information they are seeking after hitting a “dead end.”

3. Majority of customers that call to cancel have conducted 0 searches after their initial search.

Ideation

I facilitated two 1.5-hour workshops with members across departments to identify points of opportunity in our existing services to possibly integrate the following concepts: Online Reputation, Identity Protection, Sex Offender Information, Credit Report, Dark Web Education.

The Dashboard

Users are prompted to try new search types, reflect on previous search history, and activate Identity Protection.
